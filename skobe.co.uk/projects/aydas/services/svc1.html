Vacuum and Oil Filling Process

Vacuuming is the key process to remove the moisture out of the tank and dry the transformer insulation to maintain a longer lifetime.

Pulling the full vacuum on the empty transformer tank is caused conversion of the water to vapor at a lower temperature under low pressure. The vapour is extracted out of the transformer thru the vacuum pump system.

There are several techniques to determine the moisture level of the insulation including the PIPER chart method and Dew Point method. The acceptable moisture content by volume throughout the industry is 0.5 percent for a new unit and 0.65 percent for an older unit.

After achieving the desired moisture level, then the oil can be filled to the transformer under vacuum.

Vacuuming and oil filling activities shall monitor very closely by the field technicians. Our field service team are skilled to supervise the oil plant contractors to make sure the exact procedure is followed very carefully. Our team take the oil samples for oil analysis and Dissolved Gas Analysis (DGA) after oil filling process to have a reference data of the oil before energization in case of fault diagnostics.
